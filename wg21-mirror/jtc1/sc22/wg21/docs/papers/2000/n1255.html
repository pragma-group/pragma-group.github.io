Plum noted that discussion of extensions will be suspended until after this meeting at the earliest. Discussion of the time at which extension requests will be formally considered should take place on the admin reflector.

Plum also noted that Observing members of the WG are now permitted to participate in WG meetings.

1.8 Liaison reports

Benito mentioned that C met in Japan last week. C published its standard in December 1999. The document number is ISO/IEC 9899:1999. The US has not yet adopted this standard. Benito is confident that NCITS will adopt the standard soon and make it available for download for about $20.

C has submitted a new work item proposal to SC22 and to JTC1 pertaining to embedded systems performance. Apart from the new work item, the C group's efforts are devoted to a rationale and to defect reports.

1.9 New items requiring actions from the committee

Clamage noted a change to the agenda. Tuesday afternoon at 4:00pm, the full committee will meet to discuss the progress of the various subgroups. [secretary's note: this progress report was later deemed unnecessary and canceled.]

Plum recommended that the performance subgroup meet for only two days during the week so that its members could sit in on other subgroup sessions. Martin suggested that the performance group discuss that possibility among themselves.

6. General Session

Convene at 9:10 am.

6.1 WG status and progress reports.

Core group status was presented by Miller:

    Core has moved 41 issues into Ready state (from 0), 15 issues out of Review state (into Ready state), and 15 issues out of Drafting (into Ready state). Five issues were closed as NAD, one issue was closed as a request for an extension, and one item was closed as a duplicate.
    Stroustrup's "reference to reference" proposal has been moved into Ready state.
    Issue 22 was revisited, as the resolution that was proposed in Dublin has been deemed inadequate.
    The proposed resolution to Issue 185 will occasion a change to the proposed resolution to issue 20.

Library group status was presented by Dawes:

    Library began the meeting with 33 Ready issues and 17 issues in the Review category. The WG also had 40 Open issues and 32 New issues. Of those 72 issues, 51 of them were cleared. 21 Open library issues remain. Proposed wording changes to many issues were drafted; this clears a backlog that had existed after the Kona meeting.
    The Library motions for Tokyo are identified in the document numbered 00-0027=N1250.

Plum presented Performance group status:

    Plum noted that the performance group document is available on the LAN, and that much of this material was assembled by Goldthwaite and formatted by O'Riordan. Writing assignments were made in the Performance WG earlier in the week. Kühl has noted that several members of the German NB have volunteered to contribute to the TR, and O'Riordan intends to make Performance group documents available on his web site, particularly so that individuals who are not members of the Committee, like the interested members of the German NB, may participate in the Performance group.
